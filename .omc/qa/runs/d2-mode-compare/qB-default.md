**No.** By Taleb's own definition and repeated public statements, COVID-19 was explicitly **not** a Black Swan. He called it a "White Swan."

Taleb's three-part definition of a Black Swan (from *The Black Swan*, prologue):

1. **Outlier / Rarity** — "nothing in the past can convincingly point to its possibility"
2. **Extreme impact**
3. **Retrospective predictability** — humans invent explanations after the fact, making it seem predictable in hindsight

COVID-19 fails on criterion #1 — the decisive one — and consequently fails the whole definition.

**Taleb's position** (stated March–April 2020 across BBC Radio 4, Bloomberg, and The New Yorker):

- Pandemics were statistically inevitable. Epidemiologists had been warning for years. He, Bill Gates, and Laurie Garrett had all publicly predicted one.
- He co-authored a paper on January 26, 2020 (cited at academia.edu) warning that COVID-19's spread would be "nonlinear and potentially severe."
- The Bloomberg interview headline: *"Nassim Taleb Says White Swan Coronavirus Pandemic Was Preventable"* — his own framing.
- His quote to Bloomberg: *"We issued our warning that, effectively, you should kill it in the egg. Governments did not want to spend pennies in January; now they are going to spend trillions."*

A "White Swan" in Taleb's terminology is an event with **extreme impact that is known with great certainty to eventually occur** — compatible with statistical properties, not a surprise in kind.

**The key distinction** is about the locus of failure. A Black Swan is a failure of *predictability*; COVID-19 was a failure of *institutional fragility* — the systems that knew about the risk did not act on that knowledge. That distinction is central to Taleb's framework: the question is not whether you could have predicted the exact timing and strain, but whether the *class* of event was knowable. Pandemics as a class were known, modeled, and warned about. The failure was not epistemic but institutional — a fragile system that could not absorb a shock it saw coming.

For the record, Wikipedia's article on black swan theory now explicitly cites Taleb (2020) classifying COVID-19 as a "white swan" and contrasts this with other authors who label it a black or grey swan.

### MELCHIOR-1 (Empirical Skeptic)
  **Verdict:** accept-with-discount-and-carve-out

  Evidence grade: correlation-to-experiment. The best RCTs show small positive effects
  (0.09–0.15 SD on well-being), not the life transformation the digital-detox genre claims.
  The magnitude is the weakest link — the popular narrative is a post-hoc story that overfits
  to a survivor-biased sample. Silent evidence is real: relapsers, isolators, and people whose
  professional networks decay are invisible in the testimonial literature. Skin in the game is
  lopsided — quit-pill advocates earn from your quitting without bearing the downside.

  **Carve-out:** For professionals whose field runs on-platform (marketing, VC, journalism,
  academia, developer relations, crypto), the sign may be negative. CASPER-3's barbell
  restructure is a rational Bayesian prior for everyone else — it caps professional downside
  while capturing attention upside — but it is a testable hypothesis, not a proven claim.

### BALTHASAR-2 (Tail-Risk Guardian)
  **Verdict:** accept-with-removal (adjusted toward barbell)

  Time anchor: 12 months.

  Three failure classes were enumerated. The critical path: professional network collapse
  for platform-dependent fields — severe to absorbing depending on career stage. Dependents
  and employers share that risk; the decision is not private.

  Via negativa: what must be removed is **passive algorithmic consumption** — the feed,
  the scroll, the "just check once" loophole. What is preserved: RSS, newsletters, direct
  messaging, scheduled publishing. Everything that removes the agent's attention from the
  algorithm's control is removed; everything that preserves the agent's intentional
  relationship with their network is kept.

  The barbell structure (zero passive + scheduled active) substantially reduces the
  professional-network failure mode while eliminating the iatrogenic consumption pattern.
  **Replacement activity must be pre-committed and explicit** — the absence of scrolling
  is not the cure; what fills the vacated time is the cure.
